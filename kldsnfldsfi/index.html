Some weeks later, in London...

Longlegs,

I am so Sorry to only hear now; is it the sixth day sinse All Hallows Eve and here in Torpoint this breakfasttime I heard an Irishman (this is a man from Ireland, a place across the Water) speak of you. You would prick an Ear and laugh to hear what they say but I do not. And now I know whyfore I could not find the Lockett when I left that Night; all wrapt in a Cloth ready as a Present for you when we Reached the Inn. Is it a lovely thing my Velvett to love Beauty (Beauty is a kind of prettyness, the very Best sort); but what a Complication Fuss it has Caused us!

Perhaps your News shall even reach Virginia (I have told you of Virginia, and Williamsburg) before I we Shall; there is a Ship callt the Hannah which leaves for Ireland (I have told you of Ireland above) tomorrow on the Full Moon, and it is the last Crossing of the Season; & I must think of Mary & George, with my purse is nearing Empty here, & Mr. Winslow's charity e'en Emptier (Charity is a sort of love for your fellow Christian my Velvett). You shall understand that I cannot pay any Bail (Bail is a sort of rent to keap a Person from the Gaol), not e'en anonymously (this means without them knowing it was me) as they do not Allow it for Murder. Nor can I shew myself there now for it should kill poor Mary dead and despite everything she is a good Wife and as a Christian man I cannot do it. You shall know that I cannot do it.

You have been so Clever and Faithfull to me; for to claim you are with Child shall give us a Little Time. Have I thought on it all this Night gone (I have not Slept for it); and took to my Bible which I know shall have you laughing even now; I have dreamed only of your loud Laugh this week long waiting for You; and God forgive me but did I love to stand in the stuff and damp of that dining Room that Night and pantomime pre-tend to Die with you; watching your Throat move like a Bucket up a Well as you Scream'd and Smil'd at me; so keap that Smile and know that if I take the Hannah on the morning I shall have a House for us in Williamsburg within Three Month just as you Askt for it; then Instruckt pay for a Lawyer to meet with you at Bodmin. A Lawyer is a sort of man who shall Save you; and before Long you shall be coursing home to me across that Sea; to a house Warm with Fire and Food; new Names; a new Lockett if you Wish it; Rings; Hams; books if they still Please you. What was that Rhyme you Taught to me; may you Sing it even now and think of America still.

You came on so very Well with your Reading and Writing my Velvett and the Pracktise of them, een if we never did Finish; so I know that you shall Finish with this Note Quick; and when you have must I Beg you to shew it to Noone and swallow it down. It is only a Little Paper; and do you remember when I bought you that Chestnut at the Market and you ate it in a Thrice; Butter, Paper, Skin and all

Your Warren

Serjeant,

I trust your return to London was not too Onerous; I had heard the weather has been Foul West of Winchester this past Week. I En-close a Letter that has come to me at my Offices just Ahead of your Own; sent to me by Lady Chester who had it of Sir Henry Johns (the Judge at the Bodmin Assize).

The note sent along with it tells that it was taken off a young boy who had come to visit with the Accus'd in the Gaol at Looe: he was found trying to Pass it to the Girl through the Bars. When question'd he came to tell that a Short man who went by a Mr. Warren Propositioned him in a public House (The Trap & Hound) near to Plymouth town; and paid him three Shilling to have it into Miss Thomas' hands and return to him any Reply. We were lucky the fellow Em-ployed the most Honest Urchin in all Cornwal.

I trust this shall reach you at your Lodgings before we Meet next Week; and that you shall Attend me Sooner.

Yours &c.

William Moreton, Recorder Of London
